Emma

In a two week university project called Think.Make.Start at TU Munich we as an interdisciplinary team developed a system to tackle the problem of bad presentations. We started with some literature review and found out that the main way how information is transported during a presentation is by body language and voice. The content itself is only 7%. (Rule of Mehrabian).

Then we asked 100 people what they are most annoyed by during presentations by other.

And as a conclusion we decided to develop a system that allows people to get (live) feedback for their speed, prosody, volume of their voice during a presentation.

Our system consists of a smart speaker device with a built in microphone and a light stripe for live feedback and an app that allows you to get more detailed feedback after your presentation.

So you can use our system when you are training a presentation or even during your live presentation.

Our app has more features, like a singstar version of great speeches (e.g. Steve Jobs presentation of iPhone, Martin Luther King) and special exercises to improve your individual presentation skills.

This is our team representing six different disciplines.

FC Bayern Hackdays

During a 4 day hackathon organized by FC Bayern Munich and UnternehmerTUM I took part in the DHL challenge to create a platform for a global FC Bayern family. We designed a webpage where fans of Bayern Munich can take part in different challenges and gather points. They can upload pictures with their Bayern Munich merch which are shown on an interactive worldmap depending on their current location. A Bayern Munich family all over the world connected by DHL.

Finally we also developed a VR Bayern Munich wall with pictures of fans that is transforming into a globe and finally the most know Bayern Munich slogan #miasanmia.

Nachtfalter

The task was to redesign an already existing product in a more ecological way following the Cradle-to-Cradle approach.

We decided to rethink the camping chair in the context of festivals. Most people tend to leave their chair when the festival is over and as most of the chair consists of plastic and the different parts are hard to separate, it is not very ecological.

We created Nachtfalter - a chair that can be folded out of a cartoon.

By following these six steps you can fold a nice chair with a back out of a bicycle cartoon.

And the best is you can even fold the same cartoon to a box to carry your festival equipment or a flat version if you only want to carry the chair in a more convenient way.

This chair consists of only one material, that can be 100% recycled and perfectly fits the need of a festival.

Lonely Traveler

For our one semester project we had the idea to create an app where travelers and locals are able to connect with each other through similar activities. We were inspired by Google Trips but missing the opportunity to plan real activities together.

We developed our App in an iterative process …

First we asked users how they travel, what they expect during their travels, how they interact with locals and if they want to connect with them. Based on our findings we created a first paper prototype of our app.

You can create trips to different cities and select activities you are up for. Based on the dates and activities you enter locals who are living in this city are shown. You are able to get in contact with them.
